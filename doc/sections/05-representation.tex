\section{Representation}
\label{sec:representation}

The representation module maps a normalized edge window to a latent state used
by the forecast head.  The current implementation is a self-supervised
\texttt{VICReg\_Rank4}%
\note{
  Here \texttt{Rank4} means that the implementation
  extends the original VICReg architecture by one additional tensor rank, so the
  encoder operates on rank-4 input tensors: batch, channels, sequence, features.
}
encoder trained with the VICReg objective of
Bardes, Ponce, and LeCun~\cite{bardes2022vicreg}.  VICReg trains two augmented
views of the same sample to agree while preventing collapse through variance
and covariance regularization.\\


For one sample, the representation module maps the input slice and its mask%
\note{
  Invalid entries are not treated
  as market observations; they are zeroed or excluded whenever the network forms
  time-level statistics.
}
as follows:
\[
  (\tensor{x}_{u\,v}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v})
  \longmapsto
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    \tensor{s} &= h_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v})
    \in \R^{H_x \times D_e},\\
    \tensor{z} &= \Phi_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v})
    \in \R^{D_e}.
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
\]

$\tensor{s}$ is the time-indexed representation sequence, while $\tensor{z}$ is its temporal reduction embedding. 

\subsection{Representation output}
\label{subsec:encoder-output}

Internally, this sample-level output is produced in two stages.  The encoder
first produces a time-indexed latent sequence
\[
  h_{\theta} :
  \R^{C \times H_x \times D_x}
  \times
  \{0,1\}^{C \times H_x}
  \longrightarrow
  \R^{H_x' \times D_e}.
\]
In the current architecture the temporal length is preserved, so
\[
  H_x' = H_x,
  \qquad
  \tensor{s}^{b}
  =
  h_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}^{b}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v}^{b})
  \in
  \R^{H_x \times D_e}.
\]
The slice $\tensor{s}^{b\,i} \in \R^{D_e}$ is the latent state for sample $b$ at
input position $i$.

The exported representation slice is then obtained by a temporal reducer:
\[
  r_{\theta}:
  \R^{H_x \times D_e}
  \times
  \{0,1\}^{C \times H_x}
  \longrightarrow
  \R^{D_e}.
\]
The exported representation is
\[
  \tensor{z}^{b}
  =
  \Phi_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}^{b}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v}^{b})
  =
  r_{\theta}
  \left(
    h_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}^{b}, \quad {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v}^{b}),
    {}_{x}\tensor{m}_{u\,v}^{b}
  \right)
  \in
  \R^{D_e}.
\]
A simple masked mean reducer is
\[
  \omega_{b\,i}
  =
  \mathbf{1}
  \left\{
    \sum_{c=1}^{C} {}_{x}m_{u\,v}^{b\,c\,i} > 0
  \right\},
  \qquad
  \tensor{z}^{b}
  =
  \frac{
    \sum_{i=1}^{H_x} \omega_{b\,i}\cdot\tensor{s}^{b\,i}
  }{
    \sum_{i=1}^{H_x} \omega_{b\,i}
  },
\]
whenever the denominator is positive.  Other reducers, such as a last-valid
state, attention pooling, or a learned readout, are also valid choices as long
as the output remains in $\R^{D_e}$.

For a batch, $\tensor{x}$ abbreviates the stack
$(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}^{b})_{b=1}^{B}$, so
\[
  \tensor{s}
  =
  h_{\theta}(\tensor{x},\tensor{m}_x)
  \in
  \R^{B \times H_x \times D_e},
\]
and
\[
  \tensor{z}
  =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    (\tensor{z}^{1})^{\top}\\
    \vdots\\
    (\tensor{z}^{B})^{\top}
  \end{bmatrix}
  \in
  \R^{B \times D_e}.
\]

\subsection{Network structure}
\label{subsec:network-structure}

The encoder is designed for rank-four market tensors
\[
  \tensor{x}
  =
  \left(\tensor{x}_{u\,v}^{b}\right)_{b=1}^{B}
  \in
  \R^{B \times C \times H_x \times D_x}.
\]
Let $E_c$ denote the internal channel-expansion dimension, $F$ the fused channel
dimension, and $H$ the hidden width of the temporal convolution stack.  The
network has the following shape flow.

First, invalid channel-time entries are masked:
\[
  \widetilde{x}_{u\,v}^{b\,c\,i\,j}
  =
  {}_{x}m_{u\,v}^{b\,c\,i}x_{u\,v}^{b\,c\,i\,j}.
\]
A depthwise convolution over the time axis extracts local channel-wise
patterns:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(1)}
  =
  \sigma
  \left(
    \operatorname{DWConv}_{3\times 1}(\widetilde{\tensor{x}})
  \right)
  \in
  \R^{B \times C \times H_x \times D_x},
\]
where $\sigma$ is the pointwise nonlinearity.

Next, a grouped $1\times D_x$ convolution projects each channel's feature vector
into an $E_c$-dimensional latent channel space:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(2)}
  \in
  \R^{B \times C \times H_x \times E_c}.
\]
A learned channel identity vector $\vect{\chi}_c \in \R^{E_c}$ is added:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(3)}_{b\,c\,i:}
  =
  \mat{U}^{(2)}_{b\,c\,i:}
  +
  \vect{\chi}_c.
\]
This gives the network an explicit way to distinguish, for example, the
\field{1m}, \field{1h}, and \field{1d} channels even when their normalized
feature values are similar.

The tensor is then flattened over channel and expanded-feature coordinates and
passed through a learned temporal reparameterization block:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(4)}
  =
  T_{\theta}(\mat{U}^{(3)},\tensor{m}_x)
  \in
  \R^{B \times C \times H_x \times E_c}.
\]
This block preserves the temporal length $H_x$ but allows the representation to
adjust to local changes in temporal pace.

The channel dimension is fused with a $1\times 1$ convolution:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(5)}
  =
  \sigma
  \left(
    \operatorname{Conv}_{1\times 1}^{C\rightarrow F}
    (\mat{U}^{(4)})
  \right)
  \in
  \R^{B \times F \times H_x \times E_c}.
\]
Then a linear projection maps the last coordinate from $E_c$ to $H$:
\[
  \mat{U}^{(6)}
  \in
  \R^{B \times F \times H_x \times H}.
\]
The $F$ and $H$ coordinates are flattened into convolution channels, giving
\[
  \mat{U}^{(7)}
  \in
  \R^{B \times (FH) \times H_x}.
\]
Finally, a residual dilated one-dimensional convolution stack maps this sequence
to the representation width:
\[
  \tensor{s}
  =
  \operatorname{DilatedConv}_{\theta}
  (\mat{U}^{(7)},\tensor{m}_x)^{\top}
  \in
  \R^{B \times H_x \times D_e}.
\]
The transpose notation only indicates that the final tensor is stored with time
as the middle axis and representation width as the last axis.

\subsection{VICReg training objective}
\label{subsec:vicreg-training-objective}

During self-supervised representation training, two stochastic augmented views
are generated from the same edge-labeled input batch:
\[
  \tensor{x}_{(1)}
  =
  \left(\tensor{x}_{u\,v\,(1)}^{b}\right)_{b=1}^{B},
  \quad
  \tensor{x}_{(2)}
  =
  \left(\tensor{x}_{u\,v\,(2)}^{b}\right)_{b=1}^{B},
  \quad
  (\tensor{m}_{x\,(1)},\tensor{m}_{x\,(2)})
  \sim
  \mathcal{A}(\tensor{x},\tensor{m}_x).
\]
The augmentation family $\mathcal{A}$ may include causal time warping, value
jitter, time masking, point dropping, and channel dropping.  Both views are
encoded by the same encoder:
\[
  \tensor{s}_{(1)}
  =
  h_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{(1)},\tensor{m}_{x\,(1)}),
  \qquad
  \tensor{s}_{(2)}
  =
  h_{\theta}(\tensor{x}_{(2)},\tensor{m}_{x\,(2)}).
\]

VICReg is applied after a projector MLP with projector width $D_p\in\N$:
\[
  P_{\eta}:\R^{D_e}\rightarrow\R^{D_p},
\]
which acts independently on each valid time state:
\[
  \mat{Y}_{(1)}^{b\,i}
  =
  P_{\eta}(\tensor{s}_{(1)}^{b\,i}),
  \qquad
  \mat{Y}_{(2)}^{b\,i}
  =
  P_{\eta}(\tensor{s}_{(2)}^{b\,i}).
\]
The projector is used for representation learning only.  It is not the object
consumed by the forecast head.

The VICReg loss is computed on time positions that are valid in both augmented
views.  Let
\[
  \Omega
  =
  \left\{
    (b,i)
    :
    m_{x\,(1)}^{b\,c\,i}m_{x\,(2)}^{b\,c\,i}=1
    \text{ for all } c=1,\ldots,C
  \right\},
  \qquad
  N_{\Omega}=|\Omega|.
\]
Collect the projected vectors at these positions into
\[
  \mat{Y},\mat{Y}'
  \in
  \R^{N_{\Omega}\times D_p}.
\]
For $\mat{Y}\in\R^{N_{\Omega}\times D_p}$, define the centered matrix
\[
  \widetilde{\mat{Y}}
  =
  \mat{Y}
  -
  \mathbf{1}\bar{\vect{y}}^{\top},
  \qquad
  \bar{\vect{y}}
  =
  \frac{1}{N_{\Omega}}
  \sum_{n=1}^{N_{\Omega}}
  \mat{Y}_{n:}.
\]
The sample covariance of projected coordinates is
\[
  \mat{C}(\mat{Y})
  =
  \frac{1}{N_{\Omega}-1}
  \widetilde{\mat{Y}}^{\top}
  \widetilde{\mat{Y}}
  \in
  \R^{D_p \times D_p}.
\]
Let $\gamma\in\Rpos$ be the variance floor, and let
$\lambda_{\mathrm{inv}},\lambda_{\mathrm{var}},\lambda_{\mathrm{cov}}\in
\Rnonneg$ be the term weights.  With $[a]_+=\max(a,0)$, the standard VICReg
terms are
\[
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{inv}}
  =
  \frac{1}{N_{\Omega}}
  \sum_{n=1}^{N_{\Omega}}
  \left\|
    \mat{Y}_{n:}-\mat{Y}'_{n:}
  \right\|_2^2,
\]
\[
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{var}}(\mat{Y})
  =
  \frac{1}{D_p}
  \sum_{j=1}^{D_p}
  \left[
    \gamma
    -
    \sqrt{
      \operatorname{Var}(\mat{Y}_{:j})+\eps
    }
  \right]_+,
\]
and
\[
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cov}}(\mat{Y})
  =
  \frac{1}{D_p}
  \sum_{\substack{j,k=1\\j\ne k}}^{D_p}
  \mat{C}(\mat{Y})_{j\,k}^{2}.
\]
The full self-supervised loss is
\[
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{VICReg}}
  =
  \lambda_{\mathrm{inv}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{inv}}
  +
  \lambda_{\mathrm{var}}
  \left(
    \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{var}}(\mat{Y})
    +
    \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{var}}(\mat{Y}')
  \right)
  +
  \lambda_{\mathrm{cov}}
  \left(
    \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cov}}(\mat{Y})
    +
    \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cov}}(\mat{Y}')
  \right).
\]
The invariance term makes the two views agree.  The variance term prevents
collapse to a constant representation.  The covariance term discourages
redundant projected coordinates.

After training, the forecast system uses the encoder representation
$\tensor{s}^{b}$ or its reduced slice $\tensor{z}^{b}$.  The projector output
$P_{\eta}(\cdot)$ is a training device and is not part of the edge forecast
distribution in Section~\ref{sec:edge-forecast-head}.
