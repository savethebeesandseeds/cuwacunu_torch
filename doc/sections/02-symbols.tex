\section{Standard Symbols}
\label{sec:standard-symbols}

Every reusable symbol in this draft should have a declared domain or shape
before it is used.  Unless otherwise stated, bold symbols denote named tensors
or tensor families.  For the exposed data, representation, and forecast
families in this draft, upper indices denote coordinate slots or lower-rank
slices, while lower indices are reserved for semantic labels such as the edge
label $e$, the ordered edge label $u\,v$ for $e=(u,v)$, the mask-family labels
$x$ and $f$, or augmented-view labels such as $(1)$ and $(2)$.  Plain symbols
with the same letter denote scalar
components when an additional coordinate is exposed.  Other indexed families,
such as graph assignments, predictive distributions, or implementation-local hidden
states, may keep ordinary subscripts when that is clearer.  The choice between
uppercase and lowercase letters is a notational choice here rather than a
statement about tensor rank.  Calligraphic symbols denote sets or structured
collections.
